How to design any Citizen Portal static — social posts, Meta ad campaign creative, and the video versions that follow. If you're making something a CP audience will see, start here.

Last updated: July 2, 2026

On this page
Scope

What this guide covers

One guide, three outputs.

Social media posts (organic content on CP profiles), ad statics (Meta campaign images), and video versions (animated adaptations of approved statics, produced at each ad size). The rules below apply to all three — video work inherits every decision made in the approved static it's based on.

For brand fundamentals — full color palette, segment messaging, product features, and websites — see the main CP brand guide. This page covers what you need while actually designing.

Workflow

The pipeline: static → approval → video

Design the 4:5 master

Build in Canva using the colors, typography, and copy rules on this page. Design the 4:5 (1080×1350) first — that's the version that goes through approval.

Copy check

All copy on external-facing creative goes through copy approval before design is finalized. Never invent product claims, statistics, or jurisdiction counts — use only approved copy sources.

Submit for creative approval

Send the 4:5 to Creative Direction for approval. Nothing ships — organic or paid — without it. Expect notes; revisions are normal.

Build the remaining sizes

Once the 4:5 is approved, adapt it to 1:1 and 9:16 — same copy, colors, and layout logic; don't just crop. The size goes in every filename: cp-static-spendingcallout-4x5-v1.png, -1x1-, -9x16-.

Hand off to video production

Approved statics go to the video editor, who produces animated versions at each size. The static is the source of truth — video matches its copy, colors, and layout. Changed copy = back to step 2.

Log it on the tracking sheet

Every static gets a row on the campaign tracking sheet with a link to its Google Drive file. This is how the team knows what exists and its approval status — an unlogged static doesn't exist.

Colors for statics

Authority and trust without literal red-white-and-blue. The signature move is the maroon-to-blue gradient on dark layouts; yellow is a small-dose accent, never a background.

Typography

Typography for statics

Headlines: EB Garamond (weight 600–700). Body: Outfit (weight 300–400). Both are free on Google Fonts and in Canva. The serif carries civic gravitas — public record, carved-in-stone permanence — while Outfit ties CP to the wider product family.

Copy

Copy rules — these are hard rules

CP is a tool for informed citizens, not an activist brand. Every word stays factual, empowering, and non-partisan. Copy that breaks these rules gets sent back, so check before submitting:

Always
Say "constitutional republic" · write the tagline as "Facts, not headlines" (comma required) · lead with data and what the citizen can do with it · use only approved copy sources · keep tone neutral across the political spectrum
Never
Say "democracy" · say "unlimited" in any feature or pricing context · use the "whether you're ___ or ___" sentence pattern · use emojis (Material Symbols icons only) · invent statistics or jurisdiction counts · oversell features — claims must reflect what the product reliably does today

Numbers need verification

Any count that appears in creative — jurisdictions covered, meetings processed, articles generated — must come from a verified, approved source. If you can't point to where a number came from, it doesn't go in the design.

Formats

Sizes and export checklist

Every ad concept ships as a set of three. The 4:5 is the master and goes through approval; the others are adaptations — don't just crop.

16:91920 × 1080Link cards, YouTube, wide placementsOnly when specifically requested

Readable at arm's length

Headlines must read on a phone held at normal distance. If you have to squint at 25% zoom, the type is too small or too light.

Respect the edges

Keep logos and key text away from edges — Stories/Reels UI overlays the top and bottom ~250px of 9:16. Center the message.

Less text on the image

Meta favors low-text images. Put the argument in the caption/primary text; the static carries one headline and one idea.

Size goes in every filename

cp-static-[concept]-4x5-v1.png · cp-static-[concept]-1x1-v1.png · cp-static-[concept]-9x16-v1.png — videos the same: cp-video-[concept]-9x16-v1.mp4. Full rules in the naming convention.

Where campaign files live

Ad statics, drafts, and campaign videos live in Google Drive, logged on the tracking sheet — not in the brand asset library. The asset library is reserved for finalized, reusable brand assets, and Creative Direction decides if a campaign piece gets promoted into it later.

Organic

Organic social posts

Same brand rules as ads, lighter process: posts are built from approved templates (see the template library), follow the content cadence — CP posts 3×/week (civic data Tuesday, educational Thursday, segment value prop Saturday) — and every post has a visual. No text-only posts, no freeform design.
