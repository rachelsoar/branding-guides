Copy Rules

Cross-brand writing rules and the approval status of taglines and descriptions. When copy is marked approved, use it verbatim.

Last updated: July 2, 2026

On this page
The Rules

Rules that apply everywhere

Approved copy is verbatim

Taglines, brand descriptions, and bios marked approved get copy-pasted exactly. Don't paraphrase, don't "improve," don't shorten. If it doesn't fit a character limit, ask Rachel.

Don't invent taglines

If a brand's tagline is still pending (see below), leave that space empty rather than improvising one. A missing tagline is fixable; a wrong one that spreads isn't.

Voice lives on the brand pages

Each brand sounds different. Before writing anything, read the "How [brand] sounds" section: Soar · Workplace AI · Citizen Portal

Pull from proven phrases

The live websites are pre-approved copy banks. Reusing a phrase from soar.com or workplace-ai.com is always safe; the WAI social guide collects the best ones.

Voice

Voice in one line per brand

Soar

Visionary but grounded. Warm, human, optimistic about people. Never "tech for tech's sake."

Workplace AI

High Focus: confident, direct, outcome-first. Speaks to teams losing knowledge, not to AI enthusiasts.

Citizen Portal

Factual, nonpartisan, civic. Data does the talking. Never inflammatory, never partisan framing.
