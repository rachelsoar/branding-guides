Finding an asset

Need a logo, banner, or template right now?

Every approved asset lives in the brand's Assets page — preview it, copy its permanent URL, or download it. No hunting through Drive folders. Soar assets · Workplace AI assets · Citizen Portal assets

Behind those pages: approved files live in Google Shared Drives (one per brand), which auto-sync to a CDN. Every file gets a permanent URL that never breaks — safe to use in websites, emails, and ads.

Naming

The naming pattern

brand-type-descriptor-v1.ext
Do
All lowercase · hyphens between words · brand prefix first (soar-, wai-, cp-) · version last (-v1, -v2) · rename Canva exports immediately
Don't
No spaces or special characters · no dates in filenames · never "final", "new", or "FINAL-final-2" · no default Canva export names

Organization

Folder structure

The same structure is mirrored in all three Shared Drives. Organized by asset type — how people actually look for files — never by project or department. Two levels deep, no deeper.

[Brand Shared Drive] ├── logos/ │ ├── primary/ full-color defaults │ ├── reversed/ white/light versions for dark backgrounds │ ├── lockups/ logo + tagline combinations │ └── marks/ icon-only versions ├── photography/ │ ├── original/ canva-pro/ unsplash/ openart/ model-releases/ ├── social/ │ ├── profile-photos/ banners/ ├── print/ │ ├── one-pagers/ event/ merch/ ├── video/ │ ├── intros-outros/ thumbnails/ b-roll/ ├── email/ │ ├── headers/ signatures/ └── README.md licensing cheat sheet

The two rules that keep this clean

1. Only approved, final files go in. Drafts and works-in-progress stay out until approved. If it's in the Drive, it's safe to use.
2. Where a photo came from is its folder. photography/unsplash/ means it came from Unsplash — provenance is built into the path, which matters for licensing (below).
