Gradients are a core part of the WAI look. The website uses them for hero backgrounds, section transitions, and card accents. Two colors max per gradient. Direction: 135 degrees diagonal or 180 degrees vertical. Never horizontal — it reads as a loading bar.

Voice and tone

WAI's voice is the sharp colleague who simplifies everything. Not the corporate handbook. Not the excitable startup. The person in the room who says the clear thing everyone was thinking.

The High Focus rule

All WAI copy follows the High Focus framework: communication centered on the reader, not on us. "You/your/yours" dominates the copy. "We/our/us/I" gets converted or killed.

Low Focus (about us) — don'tHigh Focus (about them) — do
"We use AI to automatically extract action items from your meetings""Your action items show up in your inbox before you close the meeting tab"
"Our platform provides searchable meeting transcripts""Find any decision from any meeting — search it like email"

Three rules

RuleWhat it means
Convert it or kill itIf a phrase is Low Focus, flip it to High Focus or delete it. No third option.
Dessert firstThe benefit to the reader goes at the top of the post, not buried at the end.
The reader is the heroThey improve. They win. They discover. The reader is the doer, not the observer.
