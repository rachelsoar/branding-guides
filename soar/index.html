Brand identity

Soar is the parent brand — an AI studio that builds products to uplift how people and organizations work. Soar is not a product itself; it's the company behind Workplace AI, Fleet of Agents, and Citizen Portal. The brand represents vision, craftsmanship, and the belief that AI should serve people, not replace them.

Founded by Paul Allen (Ancestry.com co-founder). Headquartered in Utah.

Visual direction

Soar's visual identity is built on one idea: technology that serves people should look like it belongs in their world, not in a sci-fi movie. Think open sky, warm light, natural texture. The aesthetic is expansive and grounded — never cold, never aggressive, never "tech for tech's sake."

Typography

ElementFontWeightSize
HeadingsUrbanist700-800Varies by hierarchy
Body textOutfit40017px, line-height 1.65
EyebrowsUrbanist70015px, uppercase, letter-spaced
IconsMaterial Symbols Outlined—No emojis, ever

No other fonts. Urbanist for anything that needs to feel authoritative (headings, labels, navigation). Outfit for anything that needs to feel readable (body copy, descriptions, captions). Social icons use Font Awesome only when a platform icon is needed.

Hard rules: 12px minimum font size. text-wrap: balance and text-wrap: pretty applied globally.

Voice and tone

Soar's voice is the visionary founder who speaks plainly. Ambitious but not grandiose. Confident but not arrogant. The company talks about AI the way a builder talks about their craft — with respect for the work and clarity about why it matters.

Warm — technology serves peopleCold — we don't talk about humans as "users" to optimize
Grounded — we build real things that workVaporware — we don't promise what doesn't exist yet
